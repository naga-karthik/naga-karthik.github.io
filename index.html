Enamundram Naga Karthik

At Moraine Lake, Banff

Hi there! I’m Naga, a third-year biomed PhD student at NeuroPoly, Polytechnique Montréal. I’m also affiliated to Mila - Québec AI Institute. My research mainly involves developing deep learning-based methods for medical image analysis using real-world clinical data. In particular, I am interested in the analysis of magnetic resonance (MR) images of the spinal cord. My current projects include contrast-agnostic segmentation of the spinal cord and the segmentation of lesions in spinal cord injury and multiple sclerosis (MS). I have also experimented with generative modelling, in particular, training GANs for cross-modality MR-CT synthesis and diffusion models for synthesizing spinal cord lesions. In the past, I have also worked on the application of continual/lifelong learning methods for the segmentation of brain MS lesions.

When I am not working, you’ll find me either running , hiking , or reading .
